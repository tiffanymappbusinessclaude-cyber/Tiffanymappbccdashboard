## The renewal-review call script

**Compliance notes before reading further:**
- Customers are "customers" — not "clients"
- We provide "options" — not "solutions"
- No one on the call is an "expert", "advisor", or "consultant"
- Coverage features "may" or "are designed to" — never "will" or "guaranteed to"
- We do not say "best", "cheapest", "lowest", "#1"
- Multi-line discount discovery is fine. Promising specific savings is not.

---

### Opening (15–20 seconds)

> "Hi [first name], this is [your name] from Tiffany Mapp's State Farm office in
> Jacksonville. Your auto policy is coming up for renewal on [date] and I wanted
> to take a few minutes to walk through your current coverage with you so you
> have a chance to review it before it renews. Do you have about 5 minutes?"

If yes → continue. If no → schedule a callback.

### Discovery (open-ended, no leading)

> "Has anything changed in your situation since we last spoke? Different vehicle,
> someone added or removed from your household, change in commute, new home?"

Listen. Document. Look for life-event triggers.

### Coverage review

Walk through current coverage out loud:
- Liability limits (and what they cover)
- Comprehensive / collision deductibles
- Uninsured motorist
- Rental / towing
- Any current discounts on the policy

> "Your current liability is [X/Y/Z]. Florida's minimum is [A/B]. Some customers
> with similar situations choose to carry higher limits because [reason]. Would
> you like to talk through what that would look like for your situation?"

### Multi-line discovery (if applicable)

If the customer only has auto with us:
> "I notice we have your auto but not your homeowners / renters / life. Many
> households save when those are combined. Would it be helpful to put together
> a quote to compare?"

If the customer is multi-line already — skip.

### Close

> "Based on what we talked about, here's what your renewal looks like as it stands now:
> [price]. Does that work for you, or would you like me to put together options on any
> of the items we discussed?"

If price is the concern:
> "I hear you. Let me see if there are any discounts on your policy we haven't
> taken advantage of. I'll pull that together and call you back by [time]."

**Do not promise rate reductions on the call.** Promise the callback, then deliver
options.

### Logging

End of day, log in the activity tracker:
- `renewal_touches`: count of calls made
- `renewals_retained`: customers who indicated they would have shopped or lapsed
  and stayed after the review
- `renewals_lost`: customers who said no despite the review (cancel, non-renew,
  moved to competitor)
